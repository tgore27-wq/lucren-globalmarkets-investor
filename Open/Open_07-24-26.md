## 3. Risks

- **Asia-Pacific contagion risk is not fully priced:** South Korea's KOSPI fell 5.72% — a move of that magnitude historically precedes multi-session follow-through or signals a sharp country-specific shock with global supply-chain implications (semiconductors, electronics). If the underlying catalyst persists into next week, U.S. technology and semiconductor-adjacent names face renewed downside pressure, compounding the Nasdaq's already -2.88% weekly loss.
- **Consumer Discretionary breakdown signals demand deterioration:** XLY's -4.61% open, -7.31% weekly loss, and -10.41% six-month decline represent a sustained, not episodic, weakening. At a DXY of 120.53 and 10-year yields at 4.67%, the consumer is facing dual headwinds of import-cost inflation and elevated borrowing costs; a continued deterioration in discretionary spending could broaden the equity selloff beyond growth/tech and into the wider index.
- **Strong dollar (DXY 120.53) amplifying commodity and EM pressure:** A DXY at 120.53 is historically elevated and creates compounding macro risks: it accelerates commodity price declines in USD terms (Gold -21.79% over 6 months, Silver -34.76%), tightens financial conditions in emerging markets that borrowed in dollars, and erodes U.S. multinational earnings translations. If the dollar remains at these levels into Q3 earnings season, downward EPS revisions for global-revenue companies are a meaningful risk.
